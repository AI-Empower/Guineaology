Plan habitats that keep guinea pigs safe, social, and simple to clean.

Space

Start at 120 cm by 60 cm for a bonded pair and add 30 cm width for each additional pig.

Zones

Divide for hay, lounge, zoomies, and litter to reduce guarding and keep traffic flowing.

Layers

Stack absorbent pads, fleece, and rugs for quick swaps without throwing off airflow.

Key principles

  • Double the hideouts so timid pigs decompress without guarding.
  • Keep ramps shallow with textured grip for safe loft access.
  • Route cords, plants, and hazards outside the play zone.
  • Schedule floor time even with roomy cages to maintain muscle tone.

Bedding toolkit

Layer textures to keep feet healthy and air fresh.

Fleece over absorbent base

Layer washable fleece on top of two absorbent pads; sweep wet spots twice daily.

Paper bedding

Use 5–8 cm depth of dust-free paper; replace fully every 5–7 days in large habitats.

Kiln-dried pine

Only kiln-dried shavings; mix with paper bedding to extend freshness and reduce odours.

Hay loft

Dedicated hay loft catches crumbs and keeps sleeping zones cleaner and drier.

Climate guardrails

Keep airflow gentle, humidity stable, and paws dry.

Guinea pigs are built for cool, consistent climates. Use these guardrails to avoid respiratory flare-ups and keep bedding fresher for longer.

Temperature

Hold 18-24 °C (65-75 °F). Use fans indirectly and insulate from drafts to protect delicate respiratory systems.

Humidity

Target 40-70%. Track with a hygrometer and adjust using a humidifier or dehumidifier.

Air quality

Ventilate briefly with windows or air purifiers while shielding the habitat from direct gusts and aerosols.

Cleaning cadence that keeps paws healthy.

Break cleaning into daily, weekly, and monthly passes so habitats stay breathable without derailing your weekend. Keep a spare bedding set sealed and ready for quick swaps.

Daily

  • •Spot clean wet bedding
  • •Refill hay grids
  • •Wipe food crock and bottle spouts

Weekly

  • •Wash fleece liners on hot
  • •Disinfect coroplast with vet-safe cleaner
  • •Rotate hides and tunnels

Monthly

  • •Deep clean playpen panels
  • •Check for chewing wear
  • •Replace chewed tunnels or beds
