Hay first

Unlimited timothy or meadow hay keeps teeth worn, digestion steady, and boredom low.

Pellet limits

Offer 1/8 cup of plain timothy pellets per adult pig each day to prevent weight gain.

Vitamin C focus

Rotate peppers, leafy greens, and herbs to deliver 25-50 mg of vitamin C without excess calcium.

Quick facts

  • Offer hay in at least two stations so timid pigs can snack without conflict.
  • Log weights weekly and flag any 85 g change for exotic vet review.
  • Serve veggies damp to support hydration and rinse off potential pesticides.
  • Keep a backup hay bale sealed and dated to avoid emergency shortages.

Veggies

Split into at least two servings to protect tummies and ensure vitamin C absorption.

Treat fruits

2 tbsp / week

Offer no more than two small fruit servings per week to avoid excess sugar.

Vitamin C goal

50 mg

Choose veg that deliver 20–30 mg per pig daily; supplement if intake is uncertain.

Feeding routine

Keep meals consistent with a simple schedule.

Guinea pigs rely on routine. Spread vitamin C, fibre, and hydration through the day for steady energy.

Morning

Refill water, refresh hay, and serve half the vegetables for the day.

Midday

Spot clean bowls, add a light hydration snack, and rotate forage toys.

Evening

Serve the remaining vegetables, measure pellets, and note appetite or droppings.

Herbs to rotate

Rotate fresh herbs to add variety without adding too much calcium. Rinse and dry before serving.

  • Cilantro
  • Coriander
  • Basil
  • Dill
  • Mint
  • Rosemary (tiny sprigs)

Foods to limit or avoid

Guinea pig digestive systems are sensitive. Keep this watch list visible on the fridge so every helper knows what stays off the menu.

  • !Iceberg lettuce (minimal nutrients, watery stools)
  • !Onion family plants
  • !Potatoes or potato foliage
  • !Seeds, nuts, and dairy
  • !High-sugar treats more than twice weekly
  • !Alfalfa pellets for adult pigs
